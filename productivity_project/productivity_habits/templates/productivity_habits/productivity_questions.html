Productivity Questions

Based on Ali Abdaal's 6 key habits for a more productive year, here are guided questions to help you establish these habits in your life. You can use these questions at different intervals to reflect on your productivity journey.

Every 90 days, take time to set 3-4 meaningful goals (Quarterly Quests) that you want to accomplish.

Habit #1: Quarterly Quests

Instead of annual goals, focus on 90-day projects that are easier to visualize and accomplish.

Work Quests
  1. What are 2-3 work projects that would significantly move the needle if completed in the next 90 days?
  2. What skills do you want to develop in the next quarter that would enhance your career or business?
  3. Is there a specific area of your work that needs focused attention right now?
  4. What is one project that feels important but not urgent that you should prioritize?
Life Quests
  1. What area of your personal life would benefit from intentional focus over the next 90 days?
  2. Is there a habit you want to build or break in the next quarter?
  3. What relationships would you like to strengthen in the coming months?
  4. What personal project has been on your mind that you can break down into a 90-day plan?
Execution Questions
  1. What milestones would indicate progress for each quest?
  2. What specific actions will you take in the first week to build momentum?
  3. How will you track your progress on these quests?
  4. What potential obstacles might arise, and how will you address them?
Create New Quarterly Quest

Each month, evaluate your progress and adjust your approach as needed.

Monthly Review Questions
Quarterly Quest Progress
  1. How are you progressing on each of your quarterly quests? Are you on track?
  2. Which quest has seen the most progress? What contributed to that success?
  3. Which quest has seen the least progress? What obstacles are you facing?
  4. Do any quests need to be adjusted based on new information or circumstances?
Habit Implementation
  1. How consistent have you been with your weekly resets this month?
  2. How many days did you complete your morning manifesto?
  3. How many total focus minutes did you log this month?
  4. Have you maintained your standing order social events?
  5. How often did you practice multimodality multitasking?
Planning
  1. What are your top 3 priorities for the coming month?
  2. What habit would you like to improve most next month?
  3. Is there anything you need to stop doing to focus on your priorities?

The weekly reset is one of the most essential productivity habits. Spend 15-20 minutes each week reviewing and planning.

Habit #2: Weekly Reset

A 20-minute weekly review helps you stay on track with your priorities and intentions.

Review Questions
  1. What went well this past week? What wins can you celebrate?
  2. What challenges did you face? What did you learn?
  3. Did you make progress on your quarterly quests? How much?
  4. Did you accomplish your priorities from last week?
  5. How much focused time did you log? Was it directed at your priorities?
Planning Questions
  1. What are your top 1-3 priorities for the coming week?
  2. What specific actions will you take to make progress on your quarterly quests?
  3. What social events do you have scheduled this week?
  4. Are there any commitments or deadlines you need to prepare for?
  5. When will you schedule focus sessions to work on your priorities?
Complete Weekly Reset

Start each day with intention through your morning manifesto.

Habit #3: Morning Manifesto

A 2-minute morning check-in to set your intention for the day ahead.

  3. When will I work on this priority today?
  4. What potential distractions might arise, and how will I handle them?
  5. What will make today feel like a success?
Focus Log Questions (Habit #4)
  1. When will I schedule focused work time today?
  2. How many minutes do I aim to focus on my priorities today?
  3. What environment do I need to create for deep focus?
End of Day Reflection
  1. Did I complete today's adventure (most important task)?
  2. How many minutes of focused work did I log?
  3. What went well today?
  4. What could I improve tomorrow?

Habit #5: Standing Order Social Events

Regular social events that happen on a fixed schedule without needing to coordinate each time.

Reflection Questions
  • What weekly or biweekly social events would enrich your life?
  • Which relationships would you like to nurture through regular connection?
  • What day and time would work consistently for a standing social event?
  • What format would be most energizing for you (dinner, activities, etc.)?
Manage Social Events
Habit #6: Multimodality Multitasking

Making productive use of "dead time" by combining tasks that use different modalities.

Reflection Questions
  • What "dead time" exists in your day that could be more productive?
  • Which activities could you pair together (e.g., walking and listening to audiobooks)?
  • Could you use voice notes to capture ideas during commutes or chores?
  • What learning could you incorporate into your daily routines?
